This work models KG embeddings as functions f(Œ∏, x) instead of fixed vectors, scores triples by composing these functions, and uses Neural Network-based function spaces optimized via Neural Architecture Search. üí° The Problem & Our Solution

‚ùå Traditional Approach

Fixed vector embeddings (TransE, DistMult, ComplEx) are rigid and struggle with nuanced relation patterns in Knowledge Graphs.

‚úÖ Our Innovation

Function-based embeddings with more degrees of freedom, optimized through Neural Architecture Search for better expressivity.

üî¨ Methodology

Core Approach

  • Function Representations: Entities/relations as functions f(Œ∏, x) over polynomial, complex, or neural function spaces
  • Compositional Scoring: Triple scoring through function composition (best performing method)
  • Loss Functions: BCE with logits (most robust), L2 norm, margin ranking
  • Neural Architecture Search: Microsoft NNI for discovering optimal NN architectures

üèÜ Key Findings

  • Compositional scoring outperforms trilinear and vector triple product methods
  • BCE with logits shows best generalization across datasets
  • Complex-number function spaces excel on smaller Knowledge Graphs
  • Traditional models (DistMult/ComplEx) remain competitive on very large KGs

üéØ Why It Matters

Function-based embeddings add expressivity without hard-coding relation patterns. They capture phases/magnitudes (complex space) and non-linearities (neural space), especially effective for real-world, small to mid-scale KGs.
